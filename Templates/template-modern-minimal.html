02

Market Landscape

Understanding the current landscape informs where we invest. Three macro trends are shaping our market and creating both opportunities and threats.

01

AI-Native Competition

New entrants are building AI-first products that undercut incumbents on both price and speed. The window for differentiation is narrowing.

02

Buyer Consolidation

Buyers are consolidating vendors aggressively. They want fewer tools that do more, not more tools that each do one thing.

03

Platform Expectation Shift

The market is shifting from "best of breed" to "best of suite." Customers increasingly expect integrated platforms over point solutions. This changes our go-to-market motion, pricing strategy, and product roadmap priorities.

03

Strategic Pillars

Every initiative, investment, and hiring decision this quarter ladders up to one of three strategic pillars. If it doesn't map to a pillar, we don't do it.

Pillar 1

Product-Led Growth

Make the free tier so good that users convert themselves. Reduce friction at every step of the funnel — signup, first enrichment, integration, and upgrade. Self-serve should handle 80% of accounts under $500/mo.

Success MetricFree-to-paid conversion rate: 8%+ (currently 3.2%)
Pillar 2

Outbound Revenue Engine

Build a repeatable outbound motion that generates $300K+ pipeline per month. Hire BDR team, deploy multi-channel sequences, and establish ICP-driven account lists with clear qualification criteria.

Success MetricMonthly qualified pipeline: $300K+ by end of Q2
Pillar 3

Platform Expansion

Move beyond enrichment into adjacent workflow capabilities. Launch CRM sync, automated sequences, and intent signals. Become the platform customers don't want to leave.

Success MetricFeature adoption (2+ features): 60%+ of active accounts

Biggest Risk
Trying to do everything at once. The temptation to add initiatives mid-quarter is high. Resist it. Focus wins. If a new opportunity emerges, it displaces an existing initiative — it doesn't stack on top.
09

Governance Model

Clear governance prevents drift. These rituals ensure the strategy stays on track and issues surface early.

Weekly standup — 30 min, all initiative owners. Status, blockers, decisions needed.
Bi-weekly steering — 60 min, leadership + sponsors. KPI review, budget check, risk assessment.
Monthly business review — 90 min, full team. Lagging indicator deep dive, initiative demos.
Phase gate reviews — End of each phase. Go/no-go for next phase advancement.
Escalation path — Any blocker unresolved for 48+ hours escalates to leadership automatically.
Decision Framework
Decisions under $5K or 1-week impact: initiative owner decides. Decisions $5K-$25K or 1-month impact: pillar lead approves. Decisions $25K+ or quarter-level impact: leadership steering committee.
