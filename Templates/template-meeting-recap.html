Key Topics Discussed

Sprint Planning Priorities

15 min

Jordan walked through the proposed April sprint backlog. The team agreed to prioritize PLG onboarding v2 and HubSpot CRM sync as the two primary tracks. Email sequence builder MVP was deprioritized to May based on engineering capacity.

Discussion around whether to split the CRM sync into two phases — contacts-only first, then deals/activities. Sarah recommended the phased approach to reduce risk. Team agreed.

CRM Integration Timeline

10 min

Sarah flagged that the HubSpot API has rate-limiting constraints that were not accounted for in the original estimate. Bulk sync will require a queue-based approach. This adds approximately 2 weeks to the timeline.

"We can ship contacts sync by May 1, but the full bidirectional sync with deals and activities needs until May 15. I'd rather ship it right than ship it fast."

— Sarah Kim, Engineering Lead

Marcus raised concerns about the delay's impact on BDR onboarding. Alex suggested a workaround using CSV export in the interim. Team agreed to the phased approach.

BDR Hiring & Onboarding

10 min

Marcus updated: 2 of 3 BDR offers have been accepted, both starting April 7. Third candidate countered — Marcus is working with HR on a revised package. Onboarding playbook is finalized and ready for Day 1.

All three BDRs will go through the 30/60/90 ramp plan. First quota checkpoint at Day 30 targeting 50% of full quota.

PLG Onboarding v2

10 min

Lisa presented the redesigned onboarding flow. Key changes: interactive product tour, reduced fields in signup (email-only), and a guided first-enrichment experience that triggers within 60 seconds of account creation.

Expected impact: reduce time-to-first-enrichment from 8 minutes to under 2 minutes. Free-to-paid conversion target increases from 3.2% to 8%.
