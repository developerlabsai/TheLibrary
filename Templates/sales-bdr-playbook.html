BDR Mission & Role Overview

The Business Development Representative is the tip of the spear for StackGTM's revenue engine. Your mission is to generate qualified pipeline by identifying, engaging, and converting cold prospects into booked discovery calls with Account Executives. You are not selling the product — you are selling the meeting.

The BDR Mandate
"Open doors that don't know they need opening. Every qualified meeting you book is a future customer relationship worth $5K-$50K+ in ARR. You are the growth engine."

Role Responsibilities

  • Pipeline generation — Source and qualify outbound leads through cold calling, email, and LinkedIn
  • Account research — Use StackGTM enrichment tools to build targeted prospect lists with verified contact data
  • Multi-channel outreach — Execute structured sequences across phone, email, LinkedIn, and video
  • Meeting qualification — Apply BANT/MEDDIC criteria before booking with AEs
  • CRM hygiene — Log every touchpoint, update dispositions, and maintain data accuracy in real-time
  • Market intelligence — Feed competitive insights, objection trends, and ICP refinements back to leadership

Not every company is a good fit. StackGTM's enrichment platform delivers the most value to B2B sales teams that prospect actively and need better contact data without paying enterprise prices. Targeting the right accounts is the difference between 5% and 25% reply rates.

Disqualification Signals

  • No outbound motion — purely inbound/PLG companies with no sales team
  • Enterprise-only sales — companies selling $500K+ deals through RFPs and relationships
  • Pre-revenue startups — fewer than 10 employees, no product-market fit yet
  • Non-B2B — consumer brands, retail, restaurants, local services
Common Mistake
BDRs waste 40% of their time on accounts outside the ICP because "the title looked good." Always verify the company fits Tier 1 or Tier 2 before investing in research and outreach. A VP of Sales at a 5-person pre-revenue startup is not the same as a VP of Sales at a $20M SaaS company.

Buyer Personas

You'll engage three primary personas. Each has different pain points, priorities, and communication preferences. Adjust your messaging per persona — a one-size-fits-all approach gets ignored.

Persona 1

VP of Sales / Head of Revenue

The economic buyer. Cares about pipeline velocity, cost per lead, and rep productivity. They approve budgets and sign contracts. Typically 35-50, manages 5-30+ sellers, reports to CRO or CEO.

Pain Point"My team spends more time researching contacts than actually selling. Our data tools are expensive and the data is stale."
Persona 2

Sales Operations / Revenue Operations Manager

The technical evaluator. Cares about integrations, data quality, deduplication, and CRM hygiene. They build the workflows and influence tool selection. Typically 28-40, manages tech stack and reporting.

Pain Point"We're paying $15K/year for ZoomInfo and half the phone numbers are wrong. I need better data at a lower cost with native CRM integration."
Persona 3

SDR/BDR Manager / Sales Enablement Lead

The champion. Cares about rep efficiency, onboarding speed, and activity metrics. They feel the pain daily and advocate internally. Typically 28-38, manages 5-15 BDRs, measures time-to-first-meeting.

Pain Point"My reps are toggling between 4 different tools to find one phone number. I need something simple that just works inside Slack where we already live."

Cold Call Framework & Scripts

Cold calling is the highest-converting outbound channel when done well. A live conversation in 90 seconds accomplishes what 5 emails over 2 weeks cannot. The framework below is not a script to read verbatim — it's a structure to internalize.

The 5-Part Call Framework

  1. Pattern interrupt opener — Break the "telemarketer" pattern in the first 5 seconds. Use their name, acknowledge the cold call, ask for 30 seconds.
  2. Permission-based pitch — Deliver a 15-second value prop tied to their specific role/industry pain. Ask if it resonates.
  3. Discovery question — Ask ONE open-ended question about their current process. Let them talk. Listen for pain.
  4. Bridge to value — Connect their stated pain to how StackGTM solves it. Use a specific example or metric.
  5. Soft close — Suggest a 15-minute call, not a "demo." Lower the commitment bar.

Opening Script — VP of Sales

"Hey [Name], this is [Your Name] with StackGTM. I know this is a cold call — want to give me 30 seconds and you can decide if it's worth continuing?" [Wait for response — if yes:] "Thanks. I work with sales leaders at companies like [Similar Company] who tell me their reps are spending 30-40% of their time researching contacts instead of actually selling. We built an enrichment tool that runs through 5 data providers in Slack — reps get verified emails and phones in under 3 minutes without leaving their workflow. Quick question — how are your reps currently finding contact data today?"

Opening Script — Sales Ops

"Hey [Name], this is [Your Name] with StackGTM. Quick cold call — mind if I take 20 seconds to tell you why I'm reaching out?" [Wait for response — if yes:] "I noticed your team is using [Current Tool from enrichment data]. We've been hearing from ops teams that they're paying $10-15K/year for data that's 30-40% stale. We built a waterfall enrichment engine that checks 5 providers and only charges for verified results — most teams cut their data spend by 50-60%. Out of curiosity — how's your current data quality? Are your reps complaining about bad numbers?"

Voicemail Script (Under 20 Seconds)

"Hey [Name], [Your Name] from StackGTM. I help sales teams like yours cut enrichment costs by 50% while getting better data. Sending you a quick email now. Talk soon."
Never Do This
Don't ask "How are you today?" on a cold call. Don't say "I'm reaching out because..." Don't launch into a 60-second monologue. Don't ask "Is now a good time?" (it never is). The prospect decides in 5 seconds whether to hang up — your opener must break the pattern immediately.

LinkedIn Prospecting Playbook

LinkedIn is a trust-building channel, not a spam channel. Your profile is your landing page. Your engagement is your credibility. Your DMs are warm touches — not cold pitches. The goal is to become a familiar name before you ever ask for a meeting.

Profile Optimization Checklist

  • Headline — Lead with value, not title. "Helping B2B sales teams find better contact data in Slack" > "BDR at StackGTM"
  • Banner image — Branded StackGTM banner with one-line value prop
  • About section — First 3 lines visible without "See more." Include a CTA and your calendar link.
  • Featured section — Pin a case study, demo video, or free tool link
  • Activity — Post 2-3x per week about sales productivity, data quality, or outbound tips

Daily LinkedIn Workflow

  1. View 20-30 target prospect profiles (they see the notification)
  2. Send 10-15 personalized connection requests (reference their content, mutual connections, or company news)
  3. Engage with 5-10 prospect posts (thoughtful comments, not "Great post!")
  4. Send 5-8 DMs to accepted connections (value-first, not pitch-first)
  5. Post or share one piece of content about sales/enrichment

Connection Request Template

"Hey [Name] — saw your post about [Topic]. Really resonated with the point about [Specific Detail]. I work with sales teams on contact data quality — would love to connect and exchange ideas."

DM After Connection Accepted

"Thanks for connecting, [Name]. Not going to pitch you — just curious: how's your team currently handling contact enrichment? We've been hearing a lot of frustration about data quality from sales leaders in [Industry]. Always interested to hear different perspectives."
LinkedIn Limits
LinkedIn limits you to ~100 connection requests per week and flags accounts that send templated messages at scale. Quality over quantity. Personalize every request. If your acceptance rate drops below 30%, your messages are too generic.

Objection Handling Matrix

Objections are not rejections — they are requests for more information. The best BDRs welcome objections because they signal engagement. A prospect who says "we already have a tool" is more valuable than one who says nothing at all.

Top 10 Objections & Responses

ObjectionResponse Framework
"We already use Apollo/ZoomInfo""Totally get it — most of our customers were using Apollo when they found us. Quick question: what's your email bounce rate? If it's above 8%, our waterfall across 5 providers typically cuts that in half. Worth seeing the data side by side?"
"Not interested""Appreciate the honesty. Just out of curiosity — is that because contact data isn't a priority right now, or because you're happy with your current setup? Either way, I'll respect your time."
"Send me an email""Absolutely, I'll send that over right now. Just so I can make it relevant — what's the biggest pain with your current enrichment process? I'll tailor the email to that."
"We don't have budget""Makes sense. That's actually why a lot of teams start with our free BYOK tier — you bring your own API keys and pay nothing to us. Zero budget required. Want me to show you how that works in a quick 10-minute call?"
"Call me next quarter""Happy to. Just so I'm reaching out at the right time — what's happening next quarter that would make this a better conversation? Planning a new outbound push, or evaluating tools?"
"I need to talk to my team""Of course. Would it help if I sent a one-pager you can share? And would it make sense for me to loop in [their peer persona] directly so they can evaluate the technical side?"
"We built something in-house""That's impressive — not many teams invest in that. Curious: how many data providers does your internal tool check? We run a 5-provider waterfall and most teams tell us maintaining their internal tool costs more than the subscription."
"How are you different from Clay?""Clay is great for power users who want 75+ integrations and a spreadsheet UI. We're built for teams that want dead-simple enrichment — upload in Slack, get results in 3 minutes, zero learning curve. Different use cases. If your team has a dedicated ops person managing Clay, you're probably fine. If your reps need to self-serve, that's where we win."
"Your price seems high""What are you comparing it to? Our free tier is completely free with BYOK. Managed credits start at $29/month and most customers see ROI in the first week from time saved alone. What does your current tool cost per contact?"
"I'm too busy right now""Totally understand — end of quarter is brutal. Would a 10-minute call next Tuesday at 8am work? Before the day gets crazy? I'll keep it tight."
The Golden Rule
Never argue with an objection. Acknowledge it, ask a follow-up question, and redirect. The formula is: Acknowledge → Question → Reframe. If the prospect gives you 3 hard "no" signals, thank them and move on. Persistence is good. Pestering is not.

30/60/90 Day Ramp Plan

New BDRs are expected to reach full quota by Day 90. The ramp plan builds skills progressively — learn the product and ICP first, practice messaging second, and hit full activity volume third. Rushing to full volume without mastering the fundamentals creates bad habits.

Days 1-10
Learn: Product, ICP & Tools
Complete product certification. Shadow 10+ AE discovery calls. Master HubSpot, StackGTM enrichment, and LinkedIn Sales Nav. Memorize ICP criteria and buyer personas. Build first 50-account target list.
TARGET: Pass product knowledge assessment (80%+)
Days 11-20
Practice: Messaging & Call Blocks
Role-play cold calls with manager daily. Start calling at 50% volume (30-40 dials/day). Send first email sequences. Get first LinkedIn connections accepted. Record and review 3 calls/day with coach.
TARGET: 5 qualified meetings booked
Days 21-30
Execute: 75% Volume
Increase to 50-60 dials/day. Full email sequences running. Daily LinkedIn engagement routine. First 1:1 pipeline review with manager. Refine messaging based on objection patterns.
TARGET: 8-10 qualified meetings, 50% of full quota
Days 31-60
Scale: Full Activity Volume
60-80 dials/day. Full multi-channel sequences. Own account research and list building end-to-end. Start tracking personal conversion metrics. Weekly 1:1s shift from coaching to performance review.
TARGET: 12-15 qualified meetings, 75% of full quota
Days 61-90
Optimize: Full Quota
Full quota expectations. Identify highest-converting channels and double down. Build personal brand on LinkedIn. Mentor newer BDRs. Develop specialty vertical expertise. Prepare for AE promotion track.
TARGET: 15-20 qualified meetings, 100% of quota

Career Progression Path

The BDR role is a launchpad, not a destination. Top-performing BDRs at Dev Labs are promoted within 12-18 months. The skills you build here — cold outreach, objection handling, pipeline management, and business acumen — are the foundation for every revenue role.

Progression Ladder

1
BDR I (Months 0-6) — Learning the craft. Full ramp, quota attainment, mastering the daily cadence.
2
BDR II (Months 6-12) — Consistent performer. 100%+ quota for 3 consecutive months. Mentoring new hires.
3
Senior BDR (Months 12-18) — Top 20% performer. Owns a vertical or enterprise accounts. Trains the team on messaging.

Account Executive — Promoted to closing role. Manages full sales cycle from discovery to close.
